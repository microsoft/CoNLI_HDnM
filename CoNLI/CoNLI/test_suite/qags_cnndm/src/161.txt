Nicola sturgeon tonight backed the immediate transfer of all tax powers to holyrood in a move that threatens a multi - billion pound cut to scotland's budget. The first minister was forced to admit her mps would vote for fiscal autonomy as early as next year, despite the catastrophic collapse in north sea oil revenues. Such a move would leave a massive $ 7. 6 billion black hole in scotland's finances, triggering either higher taxes, deeper spending cuts or unsustainable borrowing levels. Nicola sturgeon said she would back calls for all tax powers to be devolved to scotland `as soon as possible', but labour leader jim murphy ( right ) warned this would leave a catastrophic hole in the country's budget.
